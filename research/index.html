Research Interests

I am broadly interested in machine learning algorithms and statistical methods, and how they can be used in a wide range of applications, such as disease diagnosis, anomaly detection, recommendation systems, dynamic pricing, matching markets and allocation of resources.

Current Work

Classification of Breathing Patterns using Machine Learning Techniques, supervised by Dr. Irena Koprinska.

We explored the possibility of diagnosing respiratory disease by classifying fluctuations in breath intervals and airway mechanics. This method offers an advantage over current diagnosis through spirometry, which is typically difficult to perform and is highly dependent on patient cooperation and effort. We hope to use this research in developing a home breathing test that classifies instances of respiratory disease.
